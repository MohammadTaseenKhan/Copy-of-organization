]]> https://nacom.org/news-1/feed/ 1 Young Leaders Event of the IPCC AR5 Launching in Bangladesh https://nacom.org/news-2/ https://nacom.org/news-2/#comments Mon, 27 Mar 2017 11:42:37 +0000 http://wp1.themexlab.com/newwp/podgorica/?p=240 The Young Leaders Event of the launching of the Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change (IPCC) Fifth Assessment Report (AR5) was held on the 8th August, 2014 at the Nabab Nawab Ali Senate Bhaban, Dhaka University. (Please see below for detailed news)
The event was organized in collaboration of IPCC, CDKN, LEAD with NACOM. The Young Leaders Event was participated by notable researchers, scientists and scientific communities of Bangladesh who are working on climate change.

The welcome speech was given by Prof. Dr. A. R. Mollah, Chairman, NACOM , with Mr. Ali Tauqeer Sheikh, Director, CDKN Asia, the welcome speech and brief introduction to the IPCC AR5. Dr. John Church, Coordinating Lead Author, presented his paper on IPCC Working Group I: The Physical Scientific Aspects of the Climate System and Climate Change: Sea Level Change while Mr. Dipal Chandra Barua, Lead Author, presented ‘IPCC AR5 Cross-cutting Investment and Finance Issues’.

Special Gust Mr. Abdullah Al Mohsin Chowdhury, Joint Secretary, Ministry of Environment and Forest pointed out that rice production may decrease at a range of 2.6 percent to 13.5 percent due to increase of 2° C creating a tremendous pressure on food security and that Sundarbans is highly vulnerable to climate change and it will be completely lost if the sea level rises up to 1meter and be a great loss of heritage, biodiversity, and fisheries resources. He urged the researchers, scientific community to use this valuable document to further enhance our understanding and develop our capabilities in battling climate change.

Chief Guest Prof. Dr. A A M S Arefin Siddique, Vice Chancellor, University of Dhaka called out for urgent action in climate change impacts, rather than taking long time as the costs would increase. He infused the importance of further research focus on sea level rise.

The chair of the session Dr. Qazi Kholiquzzaman Ahmad, Chairman, Palli Karma-Sahayak Foundation (PKSF) elaborated on the summary and Closing Remarks. The session was concluded with vote of Thanks from Dr. SM Munjurul Hannan Khan, Country Engagement Leader, CDKN.

]]> https://nacom.org/news-2/feed/ 3 IPCC AR5 Launched in Bangladesh https://nacom.org/news-3/ https://nacom.org/news-3/#respond Mon, 27 Mar 2017 11:42:03 +0000 http://wp1.themexlab.com/newwp/podgorica/?p=238 The Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change (IPCC) Fifth Assessment Report (AR5) was launched in Bangladesh on the 7th August, 2014, at the Ruposhi Bangla Hotel, Dhaka.
(Please see below for detailed news).
The program was hosted by The Planning Commission in collaboration with Ministry of Environment and Forests, Ministry of Disaster and Relief, IPCC, CDKN, LEAD and NACOM.

Mr. A H M Mustafa Kamal, FCA, MP, Hon’ble Minister, Ministry of Planning, Government of the People’s Republic of Bangladesh was present as the Chief Guest, while Mr. Md. Shafiqur Rahman Patwari, Secretary, Ministry of Environment and Forests and Dr. Mesbah ul Alam, Secretary, Ministry of Disaster Management and Relief were present as special guests. The launching session was chaired by Mr. Shamsul Alam, Member, Planning Commission, Govt. of Bangladesh.

Dr. S.M Munjurul Hannan Khan, Spokesperson, LDC Group at UNFCCC and Country Engagement Leader, CDKN provided the welcome speech while Mr. Ali Tauqeer Sheikh, Director of CDKN Asia, providing the introduction.

The technical session was chaired by Dr. Ainun Nishat. The key presenters were the authors Dr. John Church, Coordinating Lead Author, Dr. Mizan R Khan, Lead Author, and Mr. Dipal Chandra Barua, Lead Author of the IPCC AR5.

The relevance to Bangladesh in this pivotal report was discussed at length with the Honorable Minister pointing out how Bangladesh has already achieved being a role model in combatting climate change impacts. He has shared that the loss of 1% of our GDP is due to the adverse impacts of climate change and that since independence, Bangladesh has spent US$ 10 billion to mitigate it. Bangladesh National Planning Commission has taken initiatives to mainstream climate change into the ‘7th Five Year Plan’ as well as perspective plans of the country. His Excellency also mentioned that the Planning Commission is already undertaking a project called ‘“Poverty, Environment and Climate Mainstream (PECM)” to initiate climate mainstreaming in development process. And currently, a project called “Support to Sustainable and Inclusive Planning (SSIP)”’ in which climate change has been taken as an independent component of the project.

]]> https://nacom.org/news-4/feed/ 0 The workshop on Development of CMO sustainability plan and CREL’s Workplan Year 3 https://nacom.org/news-5/ https://nacom.org/news-5/#respond Mon, 27 Mar 2017 11:33:51 +0000 http://wp1.themexlab.com/newwp/podgorica/?p=234 The workshop on Development of CMO sustainability plan and CREL’s Workplan Year 3 was held on 3rd July at Hotel Sea palace, Cox’s Bazar.A total of 64 participants from FD(including 2 DFOs, 5 ACFs, 8 Range Officers) and 7 CMCs (Teknaf, Whykong, Shilkhali, Inani, Himchari, Medhakachhapia, Fashiakhali), 3 VCGs of Sonadia attended the workshop. The DCOP, Managers & RC acted as facilitator. The workshops goal, objectives, process of development of CMO sustainability plan, outline of workplan was discussed and described elaborately by CREL through power point presentation.

All CMCs took part in group works to finalize the draft CMO Sustainability plan where ACFs were given their suggestion. After presentation by all CMCs the participants took part on open discussion session effectively and gave their valuable feedback.

In the session 2, DCOP, Managers  and RC presented component wise plan for Y3.  Note was taken on participant’s valuable suggestion to incorporate it in CMO sustainability plan and Year 3 work plan.

The major recommendations were:

  • Development of more eco-tourism facilities in PAs so that local people can get profit
  • The need for setting up effective patrolling to ban forest fire, illicit felling of trees
  • The need for conducting more awareness meetings with local people
  • Conduct consultation meeting & workshop with DC, SP, Judge, Political leaders, Youths
  • The need for creating finds for CMOs
  • The need for alternative income generation for identified resources dependent people
